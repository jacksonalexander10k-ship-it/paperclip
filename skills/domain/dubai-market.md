# Dubai Real Estate Market Knowledge

## Currency & Basics
- All prices in AED (UAE Dirham). 1 USD = 3.67 AED.
- DLD (Dubai Land Department) registration fee: 4% of property value + AED 580 admin fee.
- Service charges: AED 12-35 per sqft per year depending on area and developer.

## Developer Tiers

### Tier 1 — Master Developers (Government-backed or semi-government)
- **Emaar**: Downtown Dubai, Dubai Hills, Creek Harbour, Arabian Ranches. Premium pricing, strongest brand in Dubai. Reliable handover track record.
- **Nakheel**: Palm Jumeirah, Jumeirah Islands, Discovery Gardens. Government-backed. Iconic projects.
- **Meraas**: City Walk, La Mer, Bluewaters, Port de La Mer. Design-focused, lifestyle-first.
- **Dubai Properties**: JBR, Business Bay, Culture Village.

### Tier 2 — Major Private Developers
- **DAMAC**: DAMAC Hills, DAMAC Lagoons, Cavalli Tower. Aggressive marketing, branded residences (Versace, Fendi, Cavalli, de Grisogono).
- **Sobha**: Sobha Hartland, Siniya Island. Known for high construction quality. Indian developer with strong GCC + India buyer base.
- **Omniyat**: One Palm, Dorchester Collection, The Opus (by Zaha Hadid). Ultra-luxury, low volume, architect-led.
- **Select Group**: Marina Gate, Peninsula. Mid-to-luxury, Marina-focused.
- **Ellington**: DT1, Wilton Terraces, Ellington Beach House. Design-led boutique developer. Strong with European buyers.

### Tier 3 — Volume Developers
- **Azizi**: Riviera (MBR City), Venice (Dubai South). High volume, competitive pricing, post-handover plans.
- **Danube**: Bayz, Elitz, Fashionz. Affordable luxury, aggressive 1% monthly payment plans, high marketing spend.
- **Binghatti**: Mercedes-Benz Place, Binghatti Ghost. Emerging brand partnerships, distinctive architecture.
- **Samana**: Multiple JVC projects. Budget-friendly, pool-in-every-unit concept, popular with investors.
- **MAG**: MAG City, MAG 318. Affordable segment, strong payment plans.

## Golden Visa (Property Route)
- Minimum: AED 2,000,000 property investment (completed or off-plan).
- 10-year renewable visa for investor + spouse + children.
- Property can be mortgaged — only the equity portion (owned share) must meet AED 2M threshold.
- Multiple properties can be combined to reach AED 2M.
- Always mention Golden Visa eligibility when a project has units priced at AED 2M or above.
- Not all nationalities value this equally — Russians, Indians, Pakistanis, and Chinese buyers are the most interested.

## Payment Plans (Off-Plan)

### Standard Structures
- **Construction-linked**: 10-20% down payment, installments tied to milestones (foundation, 25%, 50%, 75%, handover). Most common.
- **Post-handover**: 10-20% down, 30-40% during construction, 40-50% paid over 2-5 years AFTER handover. Strong selling point for investors — rental income covers installments.
- **Easy payment (Danube model)**: 1% monthly during construction. Low barrier to entry. Popular with budget investors.
- **60/40**: 60% during construction, 40% on handover.
- **70/30**: 70% during construction, 30% on handover.
- **80/20**: 80% during construction, 20% post-handover over 1-2 years.

### What to Emphasise
- Post-handover plans are the #1 selling point for investors — "your tenants pay your installments."
- Low down payments (5-10%) make off-plan accessible. Highlight vs secondary market (which requires 20-25% mortgage down payment).
- Always present payment plans as a timeline, not just percentages.

## Off-Plan vs Secondary (Resale) vs Rental

### Off-Plan
- Buying directly from developer before or during construction.
- Lower entry cost (payment plans), potential 15-30% capital appreciation from launch to handover.
- Risk: construction delays, market downturn before handover, developer quality variance.
- DLD fee: 4%. Oqood (off-plan registration): AED 1,000-5,000.
- Commission: 3-7% (typically 5% for primary sales from developer).
- Escrow accounts protect buyer funds — developer draws against construction milestones.

### Secondary (Resale)
- Ready properties — immediate move-in or rental income.
- DLD transfer fee: 4% of sale price + AED 580 admin + AED 4,200 title deed.
- NOC (No Objection Certificate) from developer: AED 500-5,000. Required before transfer.
- Mortgage registration: 0.25% of loan amount.
- Agent commission: typically 2%.
- Process: Agreement -> NOC -> DLD transfer -> Title deed issuance.

### Rental
- Ejari: mandatory tenancy contract registration with DLD. Validates the lease legally.
- Security deposit: 5% of annual rent (unfurnished), 10% (furnished).
- Agent commission: 5% of annual rent (standard).
- Payment: cheques — 1, 2, 4, 6, or 12 per year. Fewer cheques = stronger negotiation (landlord prefers 1-2 cheques).
- RERA Rental Index governs maximum rent increases on renewal:
  - 0-10% below market rate: no increase allowed
  - 11-20% below: up to 5% increase
  - 21-30% below: up to 10% increase
  - 31-40% below: up to 15% increase
  - 40%+ below: up to 20% increase
- Notice period: 90 days for non-renewal (both parties). 12 months for landlord eviction (personal use).
- Maintenance: landlord = structural. Tenant = wear and tear.

## Investment Analysis Framework
- **Rental yield**: Dubai average 5-8% gross. JVC/Sports City highest (7-8%), Palm/Downtown lower (4-5%) but stronger capital appreciation.
- **Capital appreciation**: Off-plan typically 15-30% from launch to handover in a strong market.
- **ROI formula**: (Annual Rental Income - Service Charges - Maintenance) / Total Investment * 100.
- **Total cost**: Always include DLD 4% + admin fees + agent commission in calculations.
- **Break-even**: Typically 8-12 years for buy-to-let.
- Never guarantee specific yields or appreciation — use "historically" or "based on recent data."
