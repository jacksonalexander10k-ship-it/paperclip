# Tenant Management — Dubai Real Estate

## Screening Criteria

### Rent-to-Income Ratio
- Below 30%: APPROVE — low risk, standard lease
- 30-40%: REVIEW — moderate risk, may require guarantor or additional deposit
- Above 40%: REJECT — income insufficient for this rent level

### Required Documents
- Valid passport with residence visa
- Emirates ID (front and back)
- Employment letter (on company letterhead, dated within 30 days)
- Salary certificate or 3 months bank statements
- Previous landlord reference (optional but recommended)

### Red Flags
- Frequent job changes (< 6 months per employer)
- Income from cash-heavy businesses without bank verification
- Reluctance to provide employment documentation
- History of bounced cheques (check with previous landlord)

## Lease Onboarding
1. Tenant screening completed and approved
2. Tenancy contract signed (RERA-compliant template)
3. Security deposit collected (5% unfurnished, 10% furnished)
4. Post-dated cheques collected (1, 2, 4, or 12 per year)
5. Ejari registration completed
6. DEWA transfer arranged
7. Move-in inspection documented
8. Welcome pack provided (building rules, emergency contacts, maintenance process)

## Renewal Process
- Start renewal discussion 90 days before expiry
- Check RERA Smart Rental Index for permissible increase
- Notify tenant of proposed terms 90 days before expiry (legal requirement)
- If tenant disputes: RERA Rental Dispute Centre (RDC)
- If tenant vacates: 12-month notice required for landlord-initiated non-renewal

## Ejari Registration
- Mandatory for all Dubai tenancies
- Register within 14 days of lease signing
- Required for: DEWA connection, school enrollment, residency processing
- Annual renewal aligned with tenancy renewal
- Online via Dubai REST app or Ejari centres
